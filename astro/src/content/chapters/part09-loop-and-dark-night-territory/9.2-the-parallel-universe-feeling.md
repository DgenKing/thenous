One of the strangest and most disorienting parts of the awakening process is a persistent, nagging feeling that you have shifted realities.

It doesn't feel like a metaphor. It feels like you literally went to sleep in one universe and woke up in a slightly different parallel one. Everything looks the same, but it *feels* completely different. The rules seem to have changed. The air itself feels strange.

This can be deeply unsettling. So what is it?

-   **Did you actually jump timelines?** In a reality where consciousness collapses possibilities, did you make a choice so fundamental that you literally shifted to a new branch of reality?

-   **Has your perception just changed?** Is the world the same, but your brain has been so rewired that you are perceiving it through a completely new filter, making the familiar seem alien?

-   **Is it a psychological effect?** Psychologists have a name for this feeling: **"derealization."** They describe it as a defense mechanism when the brain is overwhelmed by stress or a flood of new information. It's a known side effect of trauma, but also of intense spiritual experiences.


The truth is, the answer might be a mix of all three. But the feeling itself is a real and common part of the journey.

It's a sign that your old, narrow reality-tunnel has been shattered. The new, wider one is still being built. You are in the middle of the renovation, and everything is covered in dust.
