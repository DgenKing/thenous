You think you are one person. You feel separate. But the math tells a different story.

Let's do some simple math with numbers so big they're hard to wrap your head around.

-   Your brain has about **86 billion neurons**. For perspective, there are about 100-400 billion stars in our entire galaxy. You have a galaxy of neurons inside your skull.

-   Each of those neurons makes thousands of connections to other neurons, creating a web of over **100 trillion synapses**. That's more connections in your single brain than there are stars in 1,000 galaxies.

Now, let's scale up.

-   There are **8 billion people** on Earth.

What happens when you multiply the power of one brain by 8 billion? You get a number so vast it's almost meaningless: hundreds of quintillions of networked neurons. A planetary-scale biological computer.

But what if it's more than just a computer? What if it's a network?

We know from quantum physics that particles can be linked across space, instantly sharing information in a phenomenon called **entanglement**. If a single human brain can exhibit quantum effects, what happens when you have 8 billion of them on the same planet?

You get a **Global Consciousness Network**.

A planetary-scale quantum system of unimaginable complexity. You aren't just a user of this network. You ARE the network. You are a node.
